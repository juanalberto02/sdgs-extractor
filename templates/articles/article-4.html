Ensuring Access to Clean Water and Sanitation

Water is essential for life—yet billions of people around the world still lack access to clean drinking water, proper sanitation, and hygiene facilities. SDG 6 aims to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. Safe water is not only a basic human right, but also a cornerstone of health, education, food security, and economic development.

Currently, over 2 billion people live in countries experiencing high water stress, and more than 1.7 billion lack access to basic sanitation. Contaminated water and poor hygiene are leading causes of preventable diseases such as diarrhea, cholera, and dysentery—especially among children in developing regions.

SDG 6 includes targets to achieve universal and equitable access to safe and affordable drinking water, end open defecation, improve water quality by reducing pollution, and increase water-use efficiency. It also emphasizes the protection and restoration of water-related ecosystems, such as rivers, wetlands, and lakes.

The challenge is not just technical—it is deeply social and political. Effective water governance requires transparent policies, strong institutions, community participation, and international cooperation. Climate change, population growth, and urbanization are putting additional pressure on limited freshwater resources, making integrated water resource management more critical than ever.

Investing in water and sanitation saves lives, improves school attendance (especially for girls), and boosts productivity. Every dollar spent on water and sanitation generates multiple returns in health, well-being, and economic growth.

Water connects everything—from ecosystems to economies. Securing this vital resource for everyone is one of the most urgent steps toward building a fair and sustainable future.
